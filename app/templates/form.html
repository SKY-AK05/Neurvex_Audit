Orchvate — Neurvex Audit

Self-assessment tool for neurodiversity inclusion maturity

Your Details

Fields marked * are required.

Section 1 — Leadership & Culture

Q1. Does your organisation have a named senior leader or executive sponsor responsible for neurodiversity inclusion?

Q2. Is neurodiversity inclusion explicitly referenced in your organisation's values, strategy, or DEI policy?

Q3. Have senior leaders received education or training on neurodiversity in the past 12 months?

Q4. Does your organisation publicly communicate its commitment to neurodiversity inclusion (e.g. website, job adverts, reports)?

Q5. Are neurodivergent employees represented in leadership or decision-making roles?

Section 2 — Recruitment & Onboarding

Q6. Are job descriptions reviewed to remove unnecessarily restrictive language or requirements?

Q7. Are alternative interview formats offered to candidates who request them (e.g. written tasks, work trials)?

Q8. Are reasonable adjustments discussed proactively during the recruitment process?

Q9. Is onboarding structured, clear, and provided in multiple formats to support different learning styles?

Q10. Are hiring managers trained to conduct inclusive, bias-aware interviews?

Section 3 — Work Environment & Adjustments

Q11. Is there a clear, accessible process for employees to request reasonable adjustments?

Q12. Are flexible working arrangements available and actively supported for neurodivergent employees?

Q13. Are managers trained to understand and implement reasonable adjustments?

Q14. Are assistive technologies or tools available to employees who need them?

Q15. Are adjustment requests handled promptly and without stigma?

Section 4 — Built Environment & Sensory

Q16. Are quiet or low-stimulation spaces available for employees who need them?

Q17. Has your organisation considered lighting, acoustics, and sensory factors in its workspace design?

Q18. Is clear, consistent wayfinding and signage in place throughout your premises?

Q19. Are employees able to personalise their workstations to meet sensory needs?

Q20. Has a sensory or environmental audit been conducted in the past two years?

Section 5 — Talent Management & Development

Q21. Are performance appraisal processes reviewed to ensure they do not disadvantage neurodivergent employees?

Q22. Do neurodivergent employees have equal access to learning and development opportunities?

Q23. Are strengths-based approaches used in talent management and career development?

Q24. Are mentoring or coaching programmes available and accessible to neurodivergent employees?

Q25. Is retention data monitored to identify whether neurodivergent employees leave at higher rates?

Section 6 — Communication & Accessibility

Q26. Are internal communications written in plain, clear language and free from jargon?

Q27. Is information provided in multiple formats (e.g. written, visual, audio) where possible?

Q28. Are meeting agendas shared in advance to support employees who benefit from preparation time?

Q29. Are digital tools and platforms used by your organisation tested for accessibility?

Q30. Are employees able to request communication adjustments (e.g. written follow-ups after verbal instructions)?

Section 7 — Products & Customer Experience

Q31. Are your customer-facing products and services designed with neurodivergent users in mind?

Q32. Are neurodivergent customers or users involved in product testing or feedback processes?

Q33. Are customer service staff trained to support neurodivergent customers effectively?

Q34. Do your digital products meet recognised accessibility standards (e.g. WCAG 2.1)?

Q35. Is there a clear process for neurodivergent customers to request adjustments or alternative formats?

Section 8 — Suppliers & Procurement

Q36. Does your procurement process include questions about suppliers' neurodiversity inclusion practices?

Q37. Are suppliers expected to meet minimum neurodiversity inclusion standards as part of your contracts?

Q38. Do you actively seek to work with neurodiversity-led or neurodiversity-friendly suppliers?

Q39. Is neurodiversity inclusion performance reviewed as part of supplier relationship management?

Q40. Does your organisation share neurodiversity inclusion best practice with its supply chain?
